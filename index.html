Massacres Recorded by CEH Per Year

The massacres overwhelmingly targeted Maya peoples. Many included not just the mass killing of defenseless people, but sadistic acts including torture, rape, and the burning of entire villages. These traumatic events left lasting devastatation in Indigenous communities, including the home regions of Claudia, Jakelin, Felipe, Juan, Wilmer, and Carlos.

The Ch'orti' region suffered some of the first massacres recorded by the CEH during the conflict period (1960–1996). During the mid 1960's, the Guatemalan army and CIA-trained death squads killed thousands of peasants in the eastern part of the country.

Though the guerrilla combatants numbered less than 500, the military counter-insurgency claimed an estimated 6,000-8,000 lives in the area, as all suspected leftists were executed with impunity. While the massacres recorded by the CEH represent only a fraction of the total killed, the testimony of survivors reveals the brutality of the "counter-terror" campaign.

State violence was deployed to dispossess Indigenous lands and destroy any resistance to extractive capitalist projects. In Q'eqchi' territory, peaceful protests of exploitative land tenure practice and mining were met with violence.

On May 29, 1978, the Army opened fire on a crowd of Q'eqchi' peasants gathered in Panzós to protest the expropriation of their land by the local planter elite. More than 50 were killed, and over the next four years the army carried out extrajudicial executions of more than 300 Q'eqchi' community organizers and ancestral authorities connected to the land dispute in the Polochic Valley.

The peak of the conflict happened in the early 1980's, under the presidency of Rios Montt.
